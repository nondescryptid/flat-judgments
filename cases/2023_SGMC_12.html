District Judge Christopher Goh Eng Chiang:

Introduction

1       The accused, Yin Siyuan, (“the accused”), a 40-year-old Chinese National, claimed trial to one outrage of modesty (“OM”) charge under s 354(1) of the Penal Code (Cap 224, 2008 Rev Ed) (“PC”). The accused, a masseur at Tang Shan TCM and Massage (“the TCM shop”) in City Square Mall located along Kitchener Road was alleged to have outraged the modesty of the 28-year-old victim (“the Victim”), who was a customer at the TCM shop.

2       At the conclusion of the trial, I convicted the accused and sentenced him to 12 months’ imprisonment and three strokes of the cane. The accused, being dissatisfied his conviction and the sentence imposed, now appeals against it.

The Charge

3       The charge faced the accused is as follows:

“ …. that you, on 3 November 2019 sometime between 11:30am and 12:30pm, at Tang Shan TCM & Massage located at 180 Kitchener Road, #B2-42, City Square Mall, Singapore, did use criminal force on (redacted), (female / then 28 years old) (“the victim”), to wit, by touching and squeezing her breast and touching her right nipple, knowing it to be likely that you would thereby outrage the modesty of the said victim, and you have thereby committed an offence punishable under s354(1) of the Penal Code (Cap 224, 2008 Re Ed).”

Facts leading up to the OM incident

5       The first time the Victim met the accused was about a week prior to the incident, on either 26 or 27 October 2019. Then, the Victim had gone for a traditional Chinese massage (“TCM”) at the TCM shop with her mother, PW2. This was the Victim’s first TCM massage[note: 1]. The Victim was assigned the accused as her masseur. This first massage proceeded without controversy, and they were both sufficiently satisfied to return to the TCM shop the following week. In fact, the Victim proceeded to book another session with the same masseur for the following week, for the same type of massage but longer in duration.[note: 2]

6       On 3 November 2019, the Victim and PW2 returned to the TCM shop for their respective appointments. After informing the counter of their reservations, the Victim proceeded to the same area where she had received her massage the previous week. However, she was told that for a longer massage, she had to go to a private room.[note: 3] She was then led to the massage room. However, she was unable to recall who brought her there. She was confused as to why the massage was to be done in a private room, but she did not enquire further. Her mother did not accompany her into the massage room.

The OM incident

7       Within the massage room, the Victim laid prone on a bed, face down on her stomach. Her face was placed in a hole. She was wearing her own clothing, namely a spaghetti black top and denim shorts.

8       The accused began massaging the Victim. According to the Victim, about ten minutes into the massage, it appeared that the accused had some trouble with her bra strap as he had been fiddling with it for some time. She asked the accused whether he wanted her to take it off, but the accused didn’t reply. The Victim therefore unclasped her bra, allowing it to fall down towards the front as she was lying prone.[note: 4]

9       The accused then asked the Victim to turn over. She did so, and while she was doing so, she removed her bra and put it on a stool near her bag.[note: 5] She then went back to the bed and laid back down on the bed, facing up.

10     The Victim was then given a towel to cover her from her chest to slightly below her knee.[note: 6] The accused then proceeded to massage the Victim from her neck to her shoulder and to her collarbone and further downwards. According to the Victim, the accused spent a lot of time on her shoulders before he moved down to the collarbone.[note: 7] The massage was done with his bare hands.

11     At around this point, the accused asked the Victim whether she was married, to which she replied that she was not. The accused then told her that she had a blocked duct on her left breast and whether she wanted to unblock it. The Victim thought that this was a strange remark. She did not initially reply, but then told him that it was okay, meaning that she did not want it, and the accused then shifted to massaging the right side of the Victim’s body and massaged the same area.[note: 8]

12     The accused then proceeded to massage the side of her right breast, close to her armpits.[note: 9] As he was doing so, the Victim felt the accused’s hand go under her blouse. She felt a bit uncomfortable, but she didn’t say anything. She only felt that the accused was out of line when she felt his bare palm brush against her nipple in what she felt like a squeeze.[note: 10] The Victim was unable to remember the duration of the squeeze. She was confused as she did not know what to expect from a TCM massage when compared with a normal massage.[note: 11] She therefore gave the accused an excuse that it was a bit painful, and the accused withdrew his hand.[note: 12] There was no request from the accused, and neither was there any agreement for the accused to brush his palm over her nipple and then to squeeze her right breast. The accused also did not explain or acknowledge why he was pressing or squeezing the Victim’s breast.

13     After the accused retracted his hand, he continued the massage. He went higher to where the collar bone was, and the massage ended with a massage to the head.

For the next six minutes or so, the Victims sent PW5 a series of messages describing what had just happen to her. [note: 17] In her messages, she told PW5 that a male masseur had touched her breast and her nipple.

Feeling angry, he rode his motorbike from his workplace at Jurong West to City Square Mall. When he was with the Victim at City Square Mall, PW3, the Victim’s sister, PW4, the Victim’s brother-in-law and the police officers arrived at the scene.

The Case for the Defence

26     The accused is a male masseuse and was employed by the TCM Shop at the material time. He had begun working at the TCM Shop sometime in March 2018 and stopped working sometime in April 2020, at the start of the circuit breaker.

27     The massage appointment for 3 November 2019 was made by the Victim’s mother the previous day. He was informed of the customer’s arrival by the receptionist. Upon his arrival at the shop, he met the Victim at the counter and brought her into the room. PW2 was seated in one of the massage chairs.

28     In the massage room, he handed the Victim a pair of blue shorts and left the room. When the Victim had finished changing, he re-entered the room. He asked the Victim to lie face down on the massage bed. As the Victim told him that she felt uncomfortable in her shoulders, he began to examine the area by pressing on her collarbone area to determine where it was painful. He then began the massage.

29     As he was massaging her back, he found that that her bra strap was causing obstruction. He then lowered the strap a little to ensure that the strap would not obstruct the massage. The Victim did not take out her bra but instead, she loosened it.[note: 19]

30     After about 10 minutes, he asked the Victim to sit up. She complained about feeling pain at both sides of her neck. The pain was on both sides of her collarbone, near her shoulder. He then asked her to lie back down on the massage bed, this time, on her back. He then started massaging her neck, and then around her collarbone, including the area below the collarbone, near the shoulder.[note: 20]

31     According to the accused, when he began to massage the right side of the Victim, he realised that the right side was more “serious” than the left as the Victim felt more pain. She told him that she also felt pain in her armpit. The accused therefore asked the Victim to raise her right arm in such a way that her right hand was behind her head. He then used his right thumb to press the Victim’s right armpit, seeking permission before he did so. At this time, the Victim asked him whether there were lumps in her breast to which he replied that that it was not possible because she was young.[note: 21] After this, he started to massage the back of her neck and helped her to relax her muscles. After helping her relax her muscles, he started massaging her head and then her abdomen. By this time, it was already about an hour and the time was almost up

32     Once the time for the massage was up, the accused left the massage room and closed the door. The Victim came out about 10 minutes later. The accused served her some tea. The accused noted that PW2 had given him a SGD10 tip. The Victim then said, “thank you” and walked out of the shop. The accused went back to tidy the massage room. And went to rest at the rest area thereafter. Where he later received a call from the receptionist informing him that the next customer was ready.

The applicable legal principles

33     As with most cases of this nature, it essentially the evidence of a victim, against that of the accused. There were no independent eyewitnesses in the present case.

34     However, where sexual offences are concerned, a complainant’s testimony alone can constitute proof beyond reasonable doubt if it is so unusually convincing to overcome any doubts that might arise from the lack of corroboration (see: Pram Nair v Public Prosecutor [2017] 2 SLR 1015 at [57] (“Pram Nair”). In other words, if the evidence of the complainant is not “unusually convincing”, the conviction on the sole basis of the complainant’s testimony would be unsafe (see: Haliffie bin Mamat v Public Prosecutor and other appeals [2016] SLR 636 at [30] (“Haliffie”).

35     The term “unusually convincing” was explained by the Court of Appeal in PP v Mohammed Liton Muhammed Syeed Mallik [2008] 1 SLR(R) 601 at [39] as –

“… In our view, therefore, the “extra something’ implied by the word “unusually” must refer to the need for the trial judge to be aware of the dangers of convicting solely on the complainant’s testimony as well as of the importance of convicting only on testimony that, when weighed against the overall backdrop of the available facts and circumstances, contains that ring of truth which leaves the court satisfied that no reasonable doubt exists in favour of the accused.”

(Emphasis added)

36     A trial judge evaluates whether this standard is met “by weighing the demeanour of the complainant alongside both the internal and external consistencies found in the complainant’s testimony” (per Chan J in Kunasekaran s/o Kalimuthu Somasundara v Public Prosecutor [2018] 4 SLR 580 at [26] (“Kunasekaran”).

37     I therefore proceeded to evaluate the evidence of the Victim to determine if her evidence was unusually convincing.

Analysis of the Evidence

38     The Prosecution’s case is that the accused had used criminal force on the Victim by touching her right nipple and squeezing her right breast. He did so with the knowledge that the act would outrage the Victim’s modesty.

Whether the Victim’s evidence was internally consistent

39     I first examined the Victim’s description as to what happened when she was at the TCM shop.

The OM incident

40     The Victim’s evidence is that the accused committed the act of molest while she was lying on her back. This was about approximately ten minutes or more into the massage. The Victim had already taken off her bra at this time. [note: 22] The accused was massaging the side of her right breast, close to her armpits. As he was doing so, she felt his hand go under her blouse. She felt a bit uncomfortable, but she didn’t say anything. She only felt that the accused was out of line when she felt his bare palm brush against her nipple and thereafter what she felt like a squeeze on her breast.

41     The Victim’s description of the molest was clear and concise. Her evidence was very compelling and believable. It does not appear to me that she was embellishing her evidence. This was despite the fact that she was distressed and emotional when she recalled the incident on the stand[note: 23].

42     I noted that she was forthright in her evidence. Despite the allegation of molest that she had made against the accused, she readily admitted that she had been satisfied with the accused’s competence the first time he had massaged her and had no issue engaging him the following week. Further, she candidly admitted that she had removed her bra on her own accord after she felt the accused fiddling with the bra strap. She was also honest enough to admit that she did not recall how long was the squeeze.

43     Further, the Victim readily admitted that she did not confront the accused immediately after the incident. This was because she was shocked, confused and didn’t know what to do. She even wondered whether what had just occurred was a special TCM way of massage, and began doubting herself as to what happened. This, in my view, was unsurprising. The Victim’s had only experienced her first TCM massage the previous week.

44     From the questions posed to the Victim by the accused, it would appear that the accused was suggesting that the Victim’s allegation of molest lacked credibility because she did not shout or stream or raise say anything when the acts were perpetrated against her. However, I was of the view that the Victim had adequately explained herself and the accused suggestion were mis-conceived. There is no definitive way in which a victim to react to a sexual offence. This has been recognised by the High Court in numerous decisions. It has been held that:

(a)      “ …. there was no standard “reasonable” yardstick to judge the response of rape victims and each case had to be decided on its specific facts.” (per Foo Chee Hock JC in PP v BDA [2018] SGHC 72 (“BDA”)at [39];

(b)      “ …. victims of sexual crimes cannot be straightjacketed in their expectations that they must act or react in a specific manner ….” (per See Kee Oon J in GBR v PP [2018] 3 SLR 2056 (“GBR”) at [20]) ; and

(c)      “ …. I accept that victims of sexual offences may not behave in a stereotypical way ….” (per Aedit Abdullah J in PP v Yue Roger Jr [2018] SGHC 125 (“Yue Roger”) at [30]).

The Victim’s immediate post OM reaction

45     The Victim’s reactions immediately after the massage, in my view, is also consistent with the fact that something had happened during the massage which greatly troubled her.

46     Almost immediately after leaving the TCM shop, she told her mother what had happened during the massage. She was feeling very uncomfortable and was crying very badly.

47     Not only did she tell PW2, but she also told her sister, PW3 and her boyfriend, PW5. Her distress was clearly evident from her text messages to PW5[note: 24], as well as from the tone of her voice when she spoke to her sister and later, her boyfriend. Her reaction to what happened was, to my mind, immediate and spontaneous. It did not look contrived or planned.

Conclusion

48     At the end of the day, I adjudged that the Victim’s evidence was internally consistent. I accepted that her actions (or lack thereof) when she was molested during the massage was due to the fact that she was confused or shocked or both.

Whether the Victim’s evidence was externally consistent

49     I next considered whether the Victim’s evidence was externally consistent. First, the Victim told three people immediately after the incident.

The Victim’s reaction immediately after them OM incident

50     The Victim’s evidence that she told her mother what had happened once they had left the TCM is confirmed by PW2. The Victim told PW2 that the masseur had brushed her breast and touched her nipples[note: 25]. PW2 told the court that, by then, the Victim was very emotional and crying badly.

51     Then, almost immediately after telling PW2, the Victim contacted PW3, her sister. Likewise, PW3 confirmed this conversation. The Victim told her that the accused had brushed her nipple and either groped or squeezed her breast. PW3 could not specifically remember the exact word she used, or which breast the Victim was referring to.

52     The Victim also texted PW5, her boyfriend. Her first message to him was timed at 1240 hours[note: 26], informing him that the masseur had touched her breast and her nipples.[note: 27] This was almost immediately after the massage had ended. The text messages are consistent with the Victim’s evidence in Court.

The Victim’s subsequent reaction to the OM incident

53     PW2 has testified to the Victim’s distraught state immediately after they left the massage, while PW3 and PW5 have all testified to the victim’s emotional state when they eventually arrived at the mall.

54     Further, as highlighted by the Prosecution[note: 28], the incident had a lasting emotional impact on the Victim:

(a)     When the Victim went on the Europe trip the following week, she would “turn very hysterical” when PW2 tapped on her shoulder. PW2 also remarked that the Victim had stopped going for massages after the incident;[note: 29]

(b)     PW3 confirmed that the Victim no longer went for massages, not even foot massages. The Victim also became more sensitive to touch after the incident, and gets jumpy when someone accidentally brushes against her in the street.[note: 30]

Conclusion

55     In conclusion, I found the Victim’s evidence to be externally consistent. Her evidence in Court as to what happened is consistent with her actions immediately after the incident, including the contemporaneous documentary evidence, namely, the text messages. The evidence of PW2, PW3 and PW5 not only supported her version of events that occurred that evening, but it also supported her evidence as to the distress that she had felt after the massage.

56     Although it was PW4, and not the Victim who made the FIR, this was understandable, in light of her emotional condition immediately after the incident i.e. that she was shocked and confused Further, that FIR was made within two hours or so after the incident and this, in my view, gave more credence to her evidence as to what happened.

Whether the accused’s evidence has raised a reasonable doubt

57     The accused’s defence is one of bare denial. While he did do the massage, he did not molest her. It is not his case that the touch was accidental. The accused did not call any other witnesses, although he had earlier indicated that he wished to call a few.

58     I did not find his evidence satisfactory. First, I accepted he Prosecution’s submission that the accused’s evidence was evasive. He may, or may not have come to court with a prepared script but very often, in cross-examination, the accused failed to answer the Prosecution’s questions. He seemed to be answering the questions that he wanted to answer and not the questions that were asked. Some examples are:

(a)     The position of the Victim when he entered the massage room, whether or not she was lying face down;[note: 31]

(b)     It took quite a while before the accused would answer the Prosecution’s question as to whether he had asked the Victim whether she was married or not, only to eventually answer “I cannot remember”.[note: 32] However, subsequently, when the question was put to him that he had asked the Victim as to her marital status, he disagreed with that statement;

59     The accused’s evidence was also at times, contradictory. For example:

(a)     when he was cross-examining the Victim, he put to the Victim that he had asked her to sit up when he entered the room, at the beginning of the massage.[note: 33] However, in his own examination-in-chief, he testified that he had asked the Victim to lie down on the massage bed.[note: 34] Eventually, he admitted that his account during the Victim’s cross-examination was not accurate;

(b)     Likewise his account as to whether he massaged the Victim’s armpits. I note that during the Victim’s cross-examination,[note: 35] he asserted that he did not massage her armpits. On the other hand, when he was giving evidence, he testified that he used his right thumb to press on her armpits.[note: 36] His explanation that he was referring to the area below the armpit was, in my view, not satisfactory.

60     In all, I found the accused evasive and his evidence unsatisfactory. Having heard his version of what happened, and his answers to the Prosecution’s questions, I adjudged that it did not raise and reasonable doubt in my mind that he committed the act of molest against the Victim. In doing so, I bore in mind that the accused was not represented by counsel.

Decision at the end of trial

61     In summary, I found the Victim’s evidence to be unusually convincing. Her evidence was, in my view, internally and externally consistent. I had no doubt in my mind that the accused had used his hand and brushed the Victim’s nipples. In addition, he had used his hand to squeeze her breast. He did this without the Victim’s consent and the accused knew that his acts were likely to outrage the Victim’s modesty.

62     On the other hand, I did not find the accused’s denial convincing. His evidence did not raise any reasonable doubt in my mind. In fact, it reinforced my belief that the touches were deliberately done. I therefore adjudged that the Prosecution had proven their case beyond a reasonable doubt.

63     Accordingly, I found the accused guilty and convicted him of the charge of OM under s 354(1) of the PC.

Submissions on Sentence

64     Having convicted the accused, I next dealt with the issue of sentence.

Prosecution’s submission

65     The Prosecution submitted that the sentencing framework for offences under s 354(1) of the PC as laid out by the High Court in Kunasekaran s/o Kalimuthu Somasundara [2018] 4 SLR 599 (“Kunasekaran”) at [49] was applicable in the present case.

66     The Prosecution submitted that this case fell within Band 2 of the Kunasekaran sentencing framework (i.e. 5 to 15 months’ imprisonment). The offence-specific aggravating factors in the present case included:

(a)      Degree of sexual exploitation. The was skin-to-skin contact with the Victim’s private parts;

(b)      Premeditation. There had been no need to conduct the massage in a private room. He found excuses to unhook her bra during the massage and sked her to turn over just 10 minutes into the massage.

(c)      Abuse of trust. She trusted the accused. She had returned to engage him after a successful first massage;

(d)      Deception. The accused touched the Victim under the guise of performing a massage and had tried to use the pretext of a blocked duct to touch her. Further, he pretended that all these were part of a “normal” massage; and

(e)      Harmed caused. The Victim was now sensitive to touches, even accidental ones. She is now averse to going for massages and to men of the accused’s nationality.

67     The Prosecution referred to a similar case of PP v Ran Weidong [2021] SGMC 85 (“Ran Weidong”) where the accused, also a masseur, was convicted of molesting his customer with skin-to-skin contact at the vulva area. The offender was convicted after trial and was sentenced to 14 months’ imprisonment and six strokes of the cane. His appeal against conviction and sentence was dismissed.

68     The Prosecution acknowledged that the accused Ran Weidong had touched the victim’s vulva (compared with brushing of the nipple and squeezing of the breast in the present case), had molested the victim for a longer period of time, and had molested the victim twice. However, it was highlighted that the present case, there was premeditation on the part pf the accused whereas the district court found none in Ren Weidong.

69     Hence, the Prosecution sought a sentence of between 12 to 14 months’ imprisonment and three strokes of the cane.

Plea in mitigation

70     In his oral plea, the accused informed the court that:

(a)     there was no premeditation;[note: 37]

(b)     he had not been working for two years and seven months, since the first Circuit Breaker;[note: 38]

(c)     the case has had an adverse effect on him mentally;[note: 39]

(d)     he has had some intestinal surgery done recently; [note: 40] and

(e)     his both his father and his wife’s father had recently passed away. However, because of the present case against him, he was unable to attend his father’s funeral; [note: 41]

Decision on sentence

Punishment prescribed by law

71     The punishment prescribed by law for a charge of OM under s 354(1) of the PC is a term of imprisonment which may extend to 2 years, or a fine, or with caning, or any such combination of such punishments.

The Kunasekaran sentencing framework

72     I agreed with the Prosecution that the Kunasekaran framework should apply in the present case. Under the first step of the framework:

(a)     The Court ascertains the gravity of the offence by examining the manner and mode in which the offence was committed (“offence-specific factors”). This entails examining:

(i)       the degree of sexual exploitation,

(ii)       the circumstances of the offence, and

(iii)       the harm caused to the victim whether physical or psychological.

(b)     After examining these factors, the Court should place the offence within the appropriate band (Kunasekaran at [49]),

Band

Sentencing Range

Factors

1

Less than 5 months’ Imprisonment

Cases at the lowest end of the spectrum, which do not

2

5 to 15 months’ imprisonment

Cases where two or more offence specific aggravating factors are present. The lower end of the band involves cases where the private parts of the victim are intruded, but there is no skin-to-skin contact. The higher end of the band involves cases where there is skin-to-skin contact with the victim’s private parts.

3

15 to 24 months’ Imprisonment

Cases involving the most serious instances of outrage of modesty



73     For the second step of the framework, the Court should consider:

(a)     the aggravating and mitigating factors that are personal to the offender (“offender-specific factors”); and

(b)     the offender-specific aggravating factors include an offender’s lack of remorse and the number of charges taken into consideration.

First Step: Offence-Specific Factors

74     In my view, the present offence fell clearly within Band 2 of the Kunasekaran sentencing framework.

75     The Prosecution had listed five offence-specific aggravating factors in the present case. I did not agree entirely totally with the Prosecution’s assessment. The following are my assessment of the five offence-specific aggravating factors cited:

(a)      Degree of sexual exploitation. I note that there was skin-to-skin contact with the Victim’s private parts. This, by itself, would be sufficient to place it in the upper end of Band 2 of the Kunasekaran sentencing framework.

However, I agreed with the Prosecution that this was not as egregious as the acts of molest committed by the offender in Ran Weidong. In the present case, the touch was limited to the breast area, whereas the offender in Ran Weidong touched the victim’s vulva twice. Further, the accused’s touch in the present was not a prolonged or sustained touch when compared with that in Ran Weidong, although in the same breath, I would hesitate to describe it as being fleeting;

(b)      Premeditation. The Prosecution has used the fact that the Victim was ushered into a private room, when it was not really necessary as the basis for submitting that there was some form of premeditation. I disagreed. Like the offender in Ran Weidong, I assessed that the accused in the present case had simply been opportunistic. It has not been shown that he was responsible for the decision to massage the Victim in the private room. This assignment could have been done by the reception, or it could have been the practice of the TCM shop to use a massage room for longer massages. In my view, the accused took the opportunity to molest the Victim because he had been assigned to perform the massage in a private room;

(c)      Abuse of trust. Like the court in Ran Weidong, I accepted that there was some degree of an abuse of trust. The Victim had allowed herself to be massaged by the accused in the manner described implied that she trusted the accused as a professional masseur, and expected that he would perform the massage in a lawful manner. This was not unexpected because she had been satisfied with his service the previous week, Therefore, by outraging her modesty, the accused had thus abused this trust;

(d)      Deception. I accepted the Prosecution’s submission that there was deception. It was under the guise of performing the massage that he had molested her;

(e)      Harm caused. Like the victim in Ran Weidong, there was no victim impact statement in the present case. However, the Victim, her mother, her sister and her boyfriend have given evidence as to how the incident had affected her.

76     In all, I found that there were at least four offence-specific aggravating factors. I was of the view that the aggravating factors cited above warranted a sentence slightly above the mid-range of Band 2 of the Kunasekaran sentencing framework.

77     In Ran Weidong, an imprisonment term of 14 months was imposed on the offence. As I had earlier mentioned, I found the facts of this case to be less egregious than that in Ran Weidong, accordingly, I adjudged that the appropriate sentence in this case was about 12 months’ imprisonment.

Second Step: Offender-Specific Factors

78     I next reviewed the mitigating factors set out by the accused (see [70]), above. I did not think they warranted any downward adjustment of the sentence.

Whether caning should be imposed

79     In PP v Chow Yee Sze [2010] SGHC (“Chow Yee Sze”) at [9], the High Court held that caning will be imposed where a victim’s private parts and sexual organs were intruded upon. This itself was cited with approval in GBR at [21].

80     In the present case, the act of molest committed by the accused involved skin-to-skin contact on the private parts of a victim, namely the breast. It comprised of two acts, the brushing of the accused’s palm over the Victim’s nipple and the squeezing of the breast, hence, I was of the view that caning would be appropriate. I adjudged that the appropriate number of strokes to be ordered was three.

The Final Sentence Imposed

81     For the above reasons, I sentenced the accused to :

12 months’ imprisonment and three strokes of the cane with effect from 9 November 2022 (date of sentence)

82     The accused, being dissatisfied with his conviction and the sentence I have imposed, has now appealed against both conviction and sentence. The sentence of imprisonment and caning has been stayed pending the outcome of the appeal.
