(C)   REASONS FOR THE SENTENCES IMPOSED

4       After careful consideration of the written submissions and replies of both parties, I imposed on the AP a sentence of 16 months’ imprisonment and 1 stroke of the cane for the charge in DAC-906389-2022 and a sentence of 30 months’ imprisonment and 2 strokes of the cane for the charge in DAC-906388-2022, with both sentences to run consecutively, resulting in a global sentence of 46 months’ imprisonment with 3 strokes of the cane to be imposed on the AP. The reasons I have given to the parties at the time of imposition of the aforesaid sentences on the AP on 9 March 2023 were as follows:

Probation inappropriate

(a)     Based on the High Court sentencing considerations in A Karthik v PP [2018] 5 SLR 1289(“Karthik”) where it was held that primacy of rehabilitation for a youthful offender may be diminished by the circumstances of the case, or eclipsed by considerations of deterrence or retribution in cases where the offence is serious or the harm caused is severe, I took the view that probation will not be appropriate for the AP and it was legally correct for the defence not to submit for rehabilitation to retain primacy and hence for probation for the AP who faced 2 proceeded charges of aggravated outrage of modesty under s 354 PC against a female victim (“V”) aged 7 to 9 years and the AP was about twice the V’s age with skin to skin contact by rubbing his penis against V’s vagina in DAC 906388-2022 and touching the V’s breasts in DAC 906389-2022 plus 3 other similar s 354 PC charges and another charge for possession of obscene films to be taken into consideration for the purpose of sentencing.

(b)     Rehabilitation is typically a key consideration in sentencing young offenders for two reasons: (i) the retrospective rationale justifies rehabilitation on account of youthful folly and inexperience at the time of the offence; and (ii) the prospective rationale justifies rehabilitation on the grounds of young offenders’ receptiveness to rehabilitation, the benefit to society from rehabilitating young offenders who have constructive years ahead of them, as well as the disproportionate suffering endured by young offenders as compared to an adult offender when exposed to punitive options (Karthik at [37]). In cases where an offender was below 21 years old at the time of the offence, but above that age by sentencing, the prospective rationale would no longer apply strongly (if at all), while the retrospective rational remains relevant (Karthik at [45]). In determining whether rehabilitation is still relevant, the court should consider the circumstances of the case, including: (i) whether there was undue delay in prosecution which prejudiced the offender; and (ii) whether there was evidence of rehabilitative progress in the intervening period (Karthik at [49] and [58]).

(c)     I rejected the defence submissions and agreed with the prosecution that in the present case, while the prospective rationale did not apply strongly and the retrospective rationale might apply to some degree as the AP was 15-17 years old at the time of committing the offences, rehabilitation was overshadowed by deterrence and retribution, for two key reasons:

(i)       The prospective rationale did not apply strongly as the AP is now 26 years old, and there was no undue delay in prosecuting him upon receipt of V’s First Information Report. While V only reported the matter about 6 years after the sexual abuse, the AP should not be given a sentencing discount purely on this basis, and V cannot be faulted for the late report, as she was young and did not know how to react then (being only 7-9 years old at the material time). The AP had also admitted to knowing that “due to V’s young age at that point of time, she would not have understood what he was doing to her” (SOF at [16] and [26]). Further, the High Court also made it clear that there was “no general rule requiring victims of sexual offences to report the offences immediately or in a timely fashion” (PP v Yue Roger Jr [2019] 3 SLR 749 (“Yue Roger (HC)”) at [30]).

(ii)       The AP was 15-17 years old at the material time and was significantly older compared to the young 7- to 9-year-old V (by about 8 years, or about twice her age). He was sufficiently mature and knowledgeable to appreciate that his “actions were wrong” (SOF at [14] and [23]).

(d)     Both parties agreed on the following sentencing framework for offences under s 354(2) as laid down by the High Court in GBR v PP [2018] 3 SLR 1048 (“GBR”) at [31] to [37] for application to the present case:

(e)     In deciding the appropriate sentencing band, regard should be given to the following three factors (GBR at [28]-[30]):

(i)        Degree of sexual exploitation: part of the victim’s body the accused touched, how the accused touched the victim, duration of the outrage of modesty. The offence is more aggravated if the victim’s private parts are touched, there is skin-to-skin contact (as opposed to touching over the clothes) and the sexual exploitation continued for a sustained period rather than a fleeting moment;

(ii)        Circumstances of the offence: These include presence of premeditation, abuse of a position of trust, and exploitation of a vulnerable victim.

(iii)        Harm caused to the victim: This includes physical or psychological harm.

(f)     For the sentence to be imposed for the charge in DAC-906389-2022, both parties are broadly in agreement in that this offence falls within the middle range of Band 2 with an indicative starting sentence of about 20-24 months’ imprisonment with the prosecution seeking 16 to 20 months’ imprisonment and 1 stroke of the cane while the defence submitted for 16 months’ imprisonment and left the decision to the Court on whether to impose caning.

(g)     I agreed with the prosecution for at least 16 months imprisonment and 1 stroke of the cane to be imposed on the AP for the charge in DAC-906389-2022 for the reasons as submitted by the prosecution based on the offence-specific factors and offender-specific mitigating factors in the present case :

Offence-specific factors

(i)        High degree of sexual exploitation: This charge was committed in block XXX Yishun Street XX (their grandfather’s house). After the AP watched pornography, he touched V’s breasts with skin-to-skin contact and also touched her vagina over her clothes first, and then with skin-to-skin contact (5th charge – TIC). The context points to a high degree of sexual exploitation: (1) The AP touched V’s breasts, which is a private area; (2) While he touched her breasts over her clothes at first, he subsequently touched her breasts under her clothes, with skin-to-skin contact, which is more egregious than touching over the clothes; (3) He then rubbed her vagina over her clothes, and then subsequently under her clothes with skin-to-skin contact (subject of the 5th TIC charge); and (4) The total duration of the molest, including the 5th TIC charge, was about 30 minutes (SOF at [14]).

(ii)        Aggravating circumstances of the offence:

(1)        Abuse of position of trust: V had a close relationship with the AP and his family members as she frequently spent time with them during that period (SOF at [8]). It was in this context that the AP could easily approach V alone in their grandfather’s house, and to ask the unsuspecting V to “try something” (SOF at [11]).

(2)        Vulnerable victim (7-9 years old): The further away V ’s age is from the stipulated age ceiling (14 years old for s 354(2) PC), the more aggravating the case would be (BLV v PP [2019]2 SLR726 [“BLV”] at [132]). Here, V was only 7-9 years old and was especially vulnerable. Given her tender age and immaturity, she “did not understand what the AP was doing to her” during the sexual abuse, and thus “did not know what to do” (SOF at [15]).

(3)        Psychological harm to victim: The AP had morally corrupted V by trying pornographic sexual acts on her. As a result of the abuse, she was “shocked and scared” and felt helpless since she “did not know what to do” (SOF at [15]). Even after 6 years from the offences, she still felt “emotional, disgusted, hopeless, and unwanted” and even had to “cut herself to manage the difficult emotions she had been feeling” (SOF at [29]).

Offender-specific mitigating factors

(h)     Factoring in the offender-specific mitigating factors where the AP showed remorse by pleading guilty; and was relatively young at 15-17 years at the material time, the indicative sentence of about 20-24 months’ imprisonment should be calibrated downwards to at least 16 months’ imprisonment.

(i)        Caning: Caning should be imposed where there was intrusion into the victim’s private parts (GBR at [31]) and the starting point for Band 2 was “at least three strokes of the cane” (GBR at [33]). Here, taking into account the circumstances, the AP’s age at the time of the offence, as well as V’s age, 1 stroke of the cane should be imposed on the AP.

(j)     While some mitigating weight can be given to the AP’s young age and plea of guilt, this should not be overshadowed by the need for deterrence. The proposed 1 stroke of the cane takes into account the AP’s young age and plea of guilt, as the starting point for Band 2 was “at least three strokes of the cane” (GBR at [33]). The proposed sentence of at least 16 months’ imprisonment and 1 stroke of the cane is supported by the facts of GBR where the facts are somewhat analogous and the sentence was 25 months’ imprisonment and 4 strokes which have been calibrated downwards to take into account the AP’s young age and plea of guilt in the present case.

(k)     For the above reasons, I imposed on the AP a sentence of 16 months imprisonment and 1 stroke of the cane for the charge in DAC-906389-2022.

Sentence for DAC-906388-2022

(l)     As regards the sentence to be imposed for the charge in DAC-906388-2022, the prosecution sought for 30-34 months’ imprisonment and 2 strokes of the cane based on the offence falling within the lower end of band 3 with an indicative starting sentence of about 36-40 months’ imprisonment and at least 6 stokes, while the defence submitted for 20 months’ imprisonment based on the offence falling within the upper range of band 2 with an indicative starting sentence of about 25-36 months’ imprisonment and at least 3 strokes, but left the decision to the Court on whether to impose caning.

(m)     I agreed with the prosecution for at least 30 months imprisonment and 2 strokes of the cane to be imposed on the AP for the charge in DAC-906388-2022 for the reasons as submitted by the prosecution based on the offence-specific factors and offender-specific aggravating & mitigating factors in the present case :

Offence-specific factors

(i)        High degree of sexual exploitation : This charge was committed in block XXX Woodlands Avenue X (the AP’s house). After the AP watched pornography, he touched V’s breasts with skin-to-skin contact (1st charge– TIC), rubbed her vagina with his finger with skin-to-skin contact (2nd charge– TIC), and then rubbed his penis against her vagina with skin-to-skin contact (3rd charge–proceeded). The context pointed to a high degree of sexual exploitation: (1) He started by touching V’s breasts over her clothes, then with skin-to-skin contact (1st charge – TIC) and rubbing her vagina over her clothes, then with skin-to-skin contact (2nd charge – TIC) for about 30 minutes; and (2) After the above acts, he got very aroused and then rubbed his erect penis against her vagina (skin-to-skin contact) for about 5 minutes (3rd proceeded charge), without a condom, and he initially also tried to insert his penis into her vagina.

(ii)        Aggravating circumstances of the offence:

(1)        Abuse of position of trust: V had a close relationship with the AP and his family members as she frequently spent time with them during that period (SOF at [8]). It was in this context that the AP could easily approach V alone in their grandfather’s house, and to ask the unsuspecting V to “try something” (SOF at [11]).

(2)        Vulnerable victim (7-9 years old): The further away V ’s age is from the stipulated age ceiling (14 years old for s 354(2) of the PC), the more aggravating the case would be (BLV at [132]). Here, V was only 7-9 years old and was especially vulnerable. Given her tender age and immaturity, she “did not understand what the AP was doing to her” during the sexual abuse, and thus “did not know what to do” (SOF at [15]).

(3)        Psychological harm to victim: The AP had morally corrupted V by trying pornographic sexual acts on her. As a result of the abuse, she was “shocked and scared” and felt helpless since she “did not know what to do” (SOF at [15]). Even after 6 years from the offences, she still felt “emotional, disgusted, hopeless, and unwanted” and even had to “cut herself to manage the difficult emotions she had been feeling” (SOF at [29]).

Offender-specific aggravating & mitigating factors

(n)     Factoring in the offender-specific aggravating and mitigating factors where apart from the proceeded charges, the same acts of touching of V’s breasts and vagina, and rubbing his penis against her vagina occurred on a few occasions between 2013 and 2014 in the AP’s house (SOF at [24]). However. the AP had showed remorse by pleading guilty and was relatively young at 15-17 years at the material time, the indicative sentence of about 36-40 months’ imprisonment should be calibrated downwards to at least 30 months’ imprisonment.

(o)      Caning: The starting point for Band 3 was “at least six strokes of the cane” (GBR at [37]). Here, taking into account the circumstances, the AP’s age at the time of the offence, as well as V’s age, 2 strokes of the cane should be imposed on the AP.

(p)     The prosecution’s sentencing position is consistent with the analogous High Court reported precedents of GBR and BLV where the sentences imposed on the offenders in the aforesaid High Court precedents were enhanced on appeal. But these High Court precedents referred to and relied upon by the prosecution have been calibrated to take into account the aggravating and mitigating factors where the indicative sentence of about 36-40 months’ imprisonment and at least 6 strokes have been calibrated downwards to at least 30 months’ imprisonment and 2 strokes in the present case :

(i)       On the facts of GBR, the High Court imposed 25 months’ imprisonment with 4 strokes on the offender for fondling his 13-year-old niece’s breasts, and for touching and licking her vagina (total 10 minutes). While the AP in our present case is younger and pleaded guilty, our case is more aggravated on the facts, as there was not only a more prolonged touching of breasts and vagina (skin-to-skin contact for 30 minutes), there was also rubbing of the AP’s penis against V’s vagina, and the attempt to insert his penis in her vagina.

(ii)       In BLV, for the 5th aggravated OM charge, the offender rubbed his penis against the victim’s vagina, and when she turned over to try to avoid contact, he rubbed his penis against her anus (BLV at [17]). The Court of Appeal imposed 4 years’ imprisonment and 6 strokes on account of the aggravating factors, including an abuse of process.

(q)     The defence reliance on the case of PP v ABO [2016] SGDC 80 (“ABO”) which was decided pre-GBR is misconceived as I accepted the prosecution submission that ABO did not support the defence sentencing position of 20 months’ imprisonment for the charge in DAC-906388-2022 (rubbing penis against vagina) for the following reasons:

(i)       In ABO, the offender (step-father) was sentenced to 2 years’ imprisonment and 3 strokes for the 3rd charge of rubbing his penis against the victim’s (F/11) vagina (skin-toskin contact). The offence occurred when the offender brought the victim along for his deliveries, and he committed the offence in the lorry when the victim was asleep. When the victim felt someone touching her vagina, she woke up and screamed and said she wanted to go home. It would appear from the judgment that the offender stopped his actions thereafter.

(ii)       In GBR at [34], the High Court had considered ABO and took the view that ABO fell within the higher end of Band 2. However, I agreed with the prosecution that the present case is more aggravated and falls within the lower end of Band 3 because:

(1)       The AP is younger and V is also younger and more vulnerable (7 to 9 years old, as compared to the 11-year-old victim in ABO.

(2)       In ABO, there is no evidence that the rubbing was prolonged as the offender stopped after the victim screamed. In the present case, the rubbing was prolonged for five minutes and there were other acts of molest committed in the same transaction (prolonged touching of V’s breasts and vagina with skin-to-skin contact for 30 minutes).

(3)       The extent of harm suffered by the victim in ABO is unclear from the judgment, apart from the fact that the victim was “frightened”. In the present case, V suffered significantly. She was shocked and scared during the offences when the AP started touching her breasts, such that she did not react. This allowed him to touch her for about 35 minutes in total, where she would have been in significant shock and fear. She also suffered after the offences as she was emotional, disgusted, hopeless, and unwanted, and had to cut herself to manage the difficult emotions.

(4)       A significant aggravating factor present in our case and which is not present in ABO is that the AP not only rubbed his penis against V’s vagina, he also attempted to insert his penis into V’s vagina. The High Court in GBR at [37(b)] acknowledged that such circumstances can fall within Band 3.

(r)     For the above reasons, I imposed on the AP a sentence of 30 months’ imprisonment and 2 strokes of the cane for the charge in DAC-906388-2022.

Global sentence

(s)     The general rule is that sentences for unrelated offences should run consecutively as an offender who commit unrelated offences should be punished separately for each offence as otherwise, it may result in the perverse outcome where a multiple offender is not punished for subsequent offending. (PP v Raveen Balakrishnan [2018] 5 SLR 799 at [41]–[46])

(t)     Given that the offences were committed on two separate occasions in two different places, both parties are in agreement for the sentence of 16 months’ imprisonment and 1 stroke of the cane imposed for the charge in DAC-906389-2022 and the sentence of 30 months’ imprisonment and 2 strokes of the cane imposed for the charge in DAC-906388-2022 to run consecutively, resulting in a global sentence of 46 months’ imprisonment with 3 strokes of the cane to be imposed on the AP.

(D)   CONCLUSION

5       In my view, the aforesaid global sentence of 46 months’ imprisonment with 3 strokes of the cane which I have imposed on the AP was not manifestly excessive or inadequate, but entirely appropriate and consistent with the principles of proportionality and totality without the effect of a crushing sentence on the AP. The AP is presently serving his sentence and has filed an appeal against sentence in person on 21 March 2023.
